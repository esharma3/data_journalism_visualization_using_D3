Data Journalism and D3

Analysis Report based on correlation between health risks and poverty, age & income

Poverty and Health Risks: TX shows the highest percent of people lacking Healthcare (25%) with poverty rate approximating 17.5%. MS and NM have the highest poverty rate of of 22% and 21% respectively but a comparatively lower percentage of Healthcare absence (19% and 16%) as compared to TX. NH has the lowest poverty rate of about 6% and Healthcare Absence Rate of 11.5%. MA has the lowest Healthcare Absence Rate of 4.5% with a poverty rate of 11.8%. WV with the highest number of smokers has 18.2% poverty rate. MS with highest poverty rate also have significantly high smoking rate of about 19% and obesity rate of about 35%.

Age and Health Risks: For majority of states, people who are lacking Healthcare fall between 35 to 45 years of age group. For TX with highest Healthcare absence rate of 25% the age group is 34 to 35 years. For MA with lowest poverty rate of 4.5%, the age group lacking Healthcare is 39.5 years. For WV, the state with highest smoking rate of 27%, the age group is 42 to 43 years. For AR with highest obesity rate of about 36%, the age group is 37.5 to 38 years.

Income and Health Risks: Median household income for all the states lie between 40k and 75k. MS has the lowest median household income of $40,000 with a poverty rate of 19%. MD has the highest median income of $74,000 with a low poverty rate of 8.5%. MA is performing good in terms of both median income of about $69,000 and a low poverty rate of 4.5%. For TX with highest Healthcare absence rate of 25%, the median income is $54,000. WV with lowest median income of $41,000 has the highest smoking rate of 27% and almost highest obesity rate of about 36%.
